==============

If the radius, r and the surface area, S of a handball are
(:math:`\lambda` + 2) cm and 64\ :math:`\pi` :math:`cm^{2}`
respectively, what is the value of :math:`\lambda`?

2.1 Sample Solution
-------------------

Given that, 

.. math::
    \begin{align*} 
    S &= 64\pi \ cm^{2}\\
    r &= \lambda + 2
    \end{align*}

Since

.. math::
    \begin{align*}
    S &= 4\pi r^{2}  \\
    4\pi r^{2} &= 64\pi \ cm^{2}\\
    \end{align*}

Substituting r = :math:`\lambda` + 2, 

.. math::
    \begin{align*}
    4\pi\ (\lambda + 2)^{2} &= 64\pi \\
    (\lambda + 2)^{2} &= 16 \\
    \end{align*}

For :math:`\lambda > 0` 

.. math::
    \begin{align*}
    \lambda + 2 &= 4 \\
    \lambda &= 2 \\
    \end{align*}
