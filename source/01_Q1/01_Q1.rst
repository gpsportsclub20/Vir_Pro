Question No.Â 1
==============

Given that 1 is one of the roots of the quadratic equation below, where
h is a constant, find the value of h.


.. math::
    \begin{equation}
    2x^{2} + hx - 12 = 0
    \end{equation}

.. raw:: html

   <hr>

1.1 Sample Solution
-------------------

Let 

.. math::
    \begin{align*}
    f(x) &= 2x^{2} + hx - 12
    \end{align*}

Given that 1.5 is one of the roots, therefore

.. math::
    \begin{align*}
    f(x=1.5) &= 0 \\
    \end{align*}

Substituting x = 1.5 to solve for h,


.. math::
    \begin{align*}
    2\ (1.5)^{2} + (1.5)\ h - 12 &= 0 \\
    2\ (2.25)+1.5\ h-12 &= 0 \\
    1.5\ h &= 7.5\\
    h &= 5\\
    \end{align*}
