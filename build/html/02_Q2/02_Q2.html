Question No. 2¶

If the radius, r and the surface area, S of a handball are (\(\lambda\) + 2) cm and 64\(\pi\) \(cm^{2}\) respectively, what is the value of \(\lambda\)?

2.1 Sample Solution¶

Given that,

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} S &= 64\pi \ cm^{2}\\ r &= \lambda + 2 \end{align*}\end{split}\]

Since

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} S &= 4\pi r^{2} \\ 4\pi r^{2} &= 64\pi \ cm^{2}\\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]

Substituting r = \(\lambda\) + 2,

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} 4\pi\ (\lambda + 2)^{2} &= 64\pi \\ (\lambda + 2)^{2} &= 16 \\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]

For \(\lambda > 0\)

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} \lambda + 2 &= 4 \\ \lambda &= 2 \\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]
