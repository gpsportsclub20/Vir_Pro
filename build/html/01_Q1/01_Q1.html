Question No. 1¶

Given that 1 is one of the roots of the quadratic equation below, where h is a constant, find the value of h.

\[\begin{equation} 2x^{2} + hx - 12 = 0 \end{equation}\]

1.1 Sample Solution¶

Let

\[\begin{align*} f(x) &= 2x^{2} + hx - 12 \end{align*}\]

Given that 1.5 is one of the roots, therefore

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} f(x=1.5) &= 0 \\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]

Substituting x = 1.5 to solve for h,

\[\begin{split}\begin{align*} 2\ (1.5)^{2} + (1.5)\ h - 12 &= 0 \\ 2\ (2.25)+1.5\ h-12 &= 0 \\ 1.5\ h &= 7.5\\ h &= 5\\ \end{align*}\end{split}\]

1.2 Visualising the quadratic equation¶

Substituting the value of h = 5 into the quadratic equation:

\[\begin{equation} f(x) = 2x^{2} + 5x - 12 \end{equation}\]
